Agriculture Management System

Btech Final Year Project

IoT Based Smart Farming Management System Using Mobile App And Rainfall Prediction Model


This system proposes an automated farming system by using different sensors and also manual control of the system through a mobile device.


The hardware part consists of an Arduino, different sensors like soil moisture sensor, temperature and humidity sensor, air quality sensor, ultrasonic sensor, motor, and a Bluetooth module. Sensors are plugged at different locations in the field for data acquisition. Using some properties of the soil such as electrical resistance and dielectric constants the soil moisture sensor measures the volumetric water content in the soil. It consists of two probes that are inserted into the soil. The temperature and humidity sensor (DHT11) is a multifunctional sensor that gives the temperature and relative humidity information at the same time. The air quality sensor (MQ135) is used to sense the quality of air in Parts per Million (PPM); that is ideal for the sensing of NH3, NOx, alcohol, benzene, smoke, CO2, etc. Acetone is an organic solvent that affects soil processes. Acetone causes membrane damage, a decrease in size and germination of various agricultural and ornamental plants.

Crop Monitoring

Using some properties of the soil such as electrical resistance and dielectric constants the soil moisture sensor measures the volumetric water content in the soil. It consists of two probes that are inserted into the soil. The temperature and humidity sensor (DHT11) is a multifunctional sensor that gives the temperature and relative humidity information at the same time. The air quality sensor (MQ135) is used to sense the quality of air in Parts per Million (PPM); that is ideal for the sensing of NH3, NOx, alcohol, benzene, smoke, CO2, etc. Acetone is an organic solvent that affects soil processes. Acetone causes membrane damage, a decrease in size and germination of various agricultural and ornamental plants. Arduino Uno (Microcontroller ATmega328P) has 14 digital input/output pins, 6 analog inputs, and a 16MHz quartz crystal. The maximum operating voltage is 5V. The Arduino is programmed using Embedded C. Arduino will analyze the real-time environment data collected using different sensors and integrate them providing a better approach in making the system automated. The Bluetooth-based android application will allow the farmer to access the real-time data, collected using different sensors and to take control over the motors whenever required. Once the soil is detected to be dry (below the threshold), the farmer will be notified about the same. Based on the essential physical parameters of the soil measured and the predicted output of the rainfall prediction model, that we are implementing as a part of this project, the farmer can decide the required quantity of water from the water reservoir to be splashed on the crops.
